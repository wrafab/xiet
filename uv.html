Sublimation: Mug

Next, we learned sublimination. Sublimination is to heat up the solid ink, turn it into gas, and put those into directly the surface. UV printing only sprays the surfave. Sublimination yields better image qualities. Traditional printing may seem pixelated. Sublimination makes the image stays better on the surface because the ink is not simply on the surface but rather into the surface. I printed a mug for my dad's 50th birthday. I downloaded a birthday banner and made several modifications. Then, I exported the file as an EPS, mirrored it, and put it on CorelDraw. I set the mug printer temperature at 400 degrees and and time at 5 minutes. I printed the banner and wrapped it around the mug. Unfortunately, I wrapped the mug upside down and the image turned out upside down. Finally, I put the mug in cold water for the sublimination to settle. Next time, I need to be more careful and make sure I wrap the mug the right way. Below is the print-in-progress and my final product.
