Laser Cutting, Engraving and Woodshop

Laser Cutting and Engraving on Wood

Our first project is a clock. My clock's inspiration came from "The Persistence of Meomory" Salvador Dali. (The the original painting below) In the painting, all the bent clocks challenge the existence of time. My clock uses living hinges so that it bends in the middle. In the lower half, it contains the dead bird from the painting. I used photoshop the crop out the bird and put it on corelDraw. In corelDraw, I put the downloaded hinges design in the middle. 12 is on the upper half of the clock and 6 is on the lower half. I used the calibrator to measure the size of the hole in the middle. One issue I encounter is that the clock doesn't bend as much as I expected because the battery takes up certain space on the back and prevents the clock from bending. Next time, I would make the hinges larger to resolve this issue.

Laser Cutting and Engraving on Leather

My second project is engraving on a wristband. Instead of using the callibrator, I used a ruler to measuer its size. Once I had the size, I made a design in photoshop. I put my name and four hearts and imported the image to corelDraw. I focused the machine and set its parameters. The result turned out to be okay, but the image should be a little smaller. I think the error occured because the ruler was not as precise as the calliberator. The lesson I learned from this project is to always use callibrator for measuring when possible.
