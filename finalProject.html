Final Project

Oven alarm glove!

For the final project, we need to combine 4 processes we learned this year and design something of our own! I made an oven alarm glove. This glove will warn people when the oven is too hot so that they won't hurt themselves because of extreme heat!! The target market is in China because traditional Chinese cuisine does not require an oven. Ovens are relatively new and people are getting used to it.

Arduino part

The most challenging part of this project is the arduino part. It took a lot of time to wire and hardware and code the software. The temp sensor initially was broken and I thought I was a problem with wiring. Electronics took a long time because it was hard to figure out which part went wrong. I planned to use a buzzer as the warning mechanism, but it's hard to demo in class using a buzzer because there's no oven down here. As a prototype, I decided to use a lcd screen to display the temperature instead. This part took 8 hours. I used 2 afternoons, one Sunday, and one class period.

Sewing

We don't have gloves in the Wang Innovation Center, so I cut a hat and sewed it into a glove! Engineers love problem-solving! The sewing part is realtively easy. The glove (made from a hat) fits my hand perfectly! This part took half an hour.

Embroidery

I embroidered "careful!" on the glove. For the embroidery machine, I used the standard font. This part took around 10 minutes.

3D printing

I 3d printed a case that perfectly fits the arduino board and the battery. I used Autodesk fusion, Makerbot to design the case. For the 3D ptiner, I did not add suppot because it wasn't necessary. The 3D printing part took 20 minutes. I extended the wire that connects the temperature sensor and the board. People can install the case on the oven and reach into the oven with the temperature-sensing glove!
